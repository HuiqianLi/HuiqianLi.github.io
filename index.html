Collaborative project with a hospital, involved in the development of endoscopic assisted diagnosis equipment, focusing on real-time highlight removal, polyp classification, and segmentation of colonoscopy images.

I completed the task of building a lightweight neural network using the PyTorch framework for polyp classification and segmentation algorithms. I developed a segmentation decoder that utilizes joint attention and obeject attention mechanisms to improve performance by incorporating multi-scale features. Additionally, I implemented a simple Global Average Pooling (GAP) operation for polyp classification and utilized weakly supervised features to assist in polyp segmentation.

On our dataset, the achieved mDice score is 82.88, the mIoU score is 75.64, and the processing speed reaches approximately 120 frames per second (FPS).

Regarding my contribution to the project, I participated as the second author in the submission of the research paper titled "Polyp Segmentation with Multi-Scale Guidance and Multi-Level Supervision." And the paper has been accepted and will be presented at ACAIT2023.
